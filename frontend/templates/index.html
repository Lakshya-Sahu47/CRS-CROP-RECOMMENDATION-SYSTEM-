About This Project

What Is AgroSense?

AgroSense is an end-to-end intelligent precision farming system developed as an EPICS (Engineering Projects in Community Service) final-year capstone project. It was built to address a critical gap in Indian agriculture: small and marginal farmers — who constitute over 86% of India's farming population — lack access to timely, data-driven agronomic guidance.

The system integrates three layers of intelligence: a hardware IoT layer (NodeMCU ESP8266 + DHT11 sensor) that continuously streams real field conditions to the cloud, a computer vision layer (CNN with MobileNetV2 backbone) that classifies soil types from photographs, and a machine learning layer (XGBoost with Optuna hyperparameter tuning) that fuses all signals to recommend the optimal crop and detect plant diseases.

Unlike generic advisory apps, AgroSense is context-aware — it adapts recommendations to the exact microclimate and soil type of each individual field, polled every 15 minutes from a physical sensor.

🎯
Problem Statement
Crop failure from poor variety selection and undetected disease costs Indian farmers ₹50,000+ crore annually. 70% of farmers rely on guesswork or outdated almanacs.
💡
Our Solution
A low-cost IoT node (under ₹500) uploads live sensor data to a Flask server running 3 AI models — giving real-time, field-specific recommendations via any web browser.
📍
Target Deployment
Madhya Pradesh, India — a state with 7.7M farming households, diverse soil types (black cotton, alluvial, red), and a high crop-failure rate during Kharif season.
🏛️
EPICS Framework
Developed under the IEEE-EPICS framework, which mandates community engagement, iterative design with farmer feedback, and measurable social impact metrics.

Core AI Modules

Three Engines, One Platform

Each module was trained, validated, and optimized independently before integration.

🌱
Crop Recommendation Engine
The core XGBoost classifier was trained on a curated agro-climatic dataset covering Indian cropping conditions. Hyperparameters were tuned using Optuna (200 trials, Bayesian optimization). The model ingests 10 features including 5 hand-crafted interaction terms — a moisture index, heat-humidity interaction, log-rainfall-humidity coupling, and cyclic month encoding using sin/cos to capture seasonal periodicity. Outputs top-3 crops with probability scores and dynamically generated agronomic explanations.

Open Advisor →
🪨
Soil Classification CNN
A MobileNetV2 (frozen ImageNet backbone) with a custom classification head: GlobalAveragePooling → BatchNorm → Dense(256, ReLU) → Dropout(0.4) → Dense(128, ReLU) → Dropout(0.3) → Softmax(7 classes). Inference uses Test-Time Augmentation (TTA) — 5 augmented forward passes (rotations, flips, zoom, brightness jitter) are averaged to boost accuracy by 1–3% over single-pass inference. Classifies 7 Indian soil types: Alluvial, Black, Clay, Laterite, Loamy, Red, and Sandy.
MobileNetV2 TTA ×5 7 Soil Classes Transfer Learning
Open Analyzer →
🔬
Plant Disease Detector
A CNN trained to classify 20 disease categories spanning 6 major crops: Rice (Bacterial Blight, Brown Spot, Leaf Smut), Wheat (Rust), Potato (Early Blight, Late Blight), Pepper (Bacterial Spot), and Tomato (8 conditions including Mosaic Virus, Yellow Leaf Curl, Spider Mites). Accepts a leaf photograph, preprocesses to 224×224 RGB, and returns the top-3 disease probabilities with severity ratings, cause analysis, and a treatment protocol including recommended fertilizer or fungicide.
